The Warehouse

Nestled in the heart of the Mission District, just a block from 16th St BART, this warehouse is more than a building—it’s a canvas. It’s funky, with homeless folks sleeping next to trash along the street, but that’s part of its realness. This place has a rich history, and I’m totally in love with it.

Here’s the plan: the first floor becomes offices for creators and doers. The second floor—right now an art coop—turns into a school. Not some stiff, boxed-in thing, but a space where kids can learn their way. In a few years, we’ll build two more floors for families to live, work, and grow together. And up top, a roof with a statue of splitting arrows—breaking the line of sight and symbolizing focus, freedom, and questioning everything.

The Farm

For a quarter or third of the year, we’ll ditch the city for a farm outside Seattle. It’s where the kids can garden hard, play wild, and soak up nature. This isn’t about running away—it’s about growing bigger. The land’s got lessons you can’t fake, and there’s nothing like it for expanding your head and heart.

Our Vision

I was homeschooled, and it messed me up in the best ways—left me curious and free. Now I want that for my kids: a mind-nutrient-rich spot where they can create, destroy, and figure shit out. Not pampered—real. A place that trusts them to find their own path, even when it’s messy.

ExitBox.how is about building that—a community where learning’s raw and alive. It’s not for everyone, and I’m cool with that. If you’re here, you probably feel it too. Let’s make it happen.
